How I got into Computer Engineering

I’m a Computer Engineering graduate with a lifelong passion for technology and science. Growing up with both of my parents as computer engineering professors, I was introduced to the world of programming and problem-solving at an early age. At just 12 years old, I began attending some of my mother’s university classes, where I discovered how much I enjoyed problem-solving thinking and building solutions through code.

As I got closer to university, I enrolled in programming courses where I developed small games using Scratch and other high-level programming languages. Seeing my ideas come to life on the screen was a defining moment for me as it transformed programming from something interesting into something pratical that I truly loved. That experience sparked a deep curiosity and a strong drive to continuously learn, improve and build pratical applications.

Since then, I’ve always envisioned myself working in Computer Engineering, building innovative solutions and tackling complex problems. I’m motivated by challenges, excited by emerging technologies, and constantly looking for opportunities to grow as a developer, engineer and person.
